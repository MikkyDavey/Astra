📘 Algebra 1 Module Overview

📦 Module 0: The Numbers in Order

🎯 Overview

In this module, students revisit the structure of the number system through historical and conceptual lenses. They build intuition for operations with integers, fractions, and decimals, using physical and visual models. The goal is to deepen understanding of number behavior to support fluency with expressions and properties. The module culminates in simplifying expressions using the properties of operations, laying the foundation for algebraic thinking.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Combining integers using number lines (Level 1 and 2)
  • Adding and subtracting integers (visual and symbolic methods)
  • Absolute value as distance from zero
  • Subtraction as adding the opposite
  • Multiplying and dividing integers
  • Multiplication and division with unit fractions
  • Identifying and ordering fractions on a number line (guided and independent)
  • Comparing and scaling fractions (scale down and up)
  • Classifying numbers as rational or irrational
  • Using exponent rules (variable-only)
  • Calculating squares, cubes, and roots
  • Approximating square roots
  • Working with scientific notation (add/subtract)
  • Reviewing and applying the three properties of numbers
  • Distributive property and combining like terms
  • Simplifying expressions with distribution and properties
  • Using modeling to compare and evaluate expressions
  • Exploring number systems historically (natural, whole, integers, rationals, irrationals)

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Human Number Line Walk – students physically model integer operations and absolute value
  • Fraction Number Line Expansion – students place new fractions and decimals onto a shared projected/magnetic number line, filling in gaps from Week 1
  • “Positive, Negative, or Both?” Sorting Task – early integer opener; group sorting challenge with integer rules and expressions
  • Notebook Graphic Organizer: “Types of Numbers” – built over time in the module, starting Week 1
  • Fraction Operations Foldable – covers both addition/subtraction (common denominators, mixed numbers) and multiplication/division (including multiplicative inverse), completed over two days
  • Multiplication & Division Models – visual and symbolic connections including multiplicative inverse
  • Distributive Property Puzzles – cut-and-match puzzles connecting area models to expressions
  • Expression Stations – rotating practice stations for combining like terms, distribution, and simplification with embedded notebook prompts
  • Comparing & Ordering Challenge – ambiguous ranking task bridging into order of operations
  • Order of Operations & Evaluating Expressions Scavenger Hunt – self-checking, collaborative challenge
  • Properties Intro with Equivalent Expressions Matching – discovery task for commutative, associative, distributive properties
  • Sell That Property Analogy + Poster Task – creative application of properties in visual format
  • Module 0 Final Challenge – group and individual multi-skill problem set

📁 Key Resources

  • DeltaMath: Integer Operations, Fractions on the Number Line, Comparing/Ordering, Scientific Notation, Properties with Algebraic Expressions, Order of Operations, Simplifying Expressions
  • Order of Operations & Evaluating Expressions Scavenger Hunt PDF
  • Properties Notes & Card Sort (adapted for 2-day use)
  • Simplification Error Analysis set
  • Sell That Property task sheet and poster template
  • Module 0 Final Challenge packet
  • Hands-on number line materials (tape, cards, flags)
  • Distributive Property puzzle set (cut-and-paste)
  • Card decks for integer games
  • Scientific notation data gallery printouts
  • Notebook scaffolds and anchor pages for operations and expressions
  • “Types of Numbers” visual organizer template
  • Integer War card sets
  • Fraction strip cutouts and tape-based number lines

🔁 Builds On

  • Middle school understanding of number systems
  • Prior exposure to integer operations, fractions, and decimals
  • Informal experience with the distributive property and combining like terms
  • Conceptual understanding of equality and basic equation structure
  • Familiarity with order of operations from middle school

➡️ Leads To

  • Algebraic properties of operations applied to real-world contexts (Module 1)
  • Simplifying expressions with variables and coefficients
  • Fluency needed for solving equations and inequalities (Module 2)
  • Structured notebook organization habits for multi-step algebra work

📅 Weekly Breakdown

  • Week 1 (Sept 1–5): Surveys, folders, placement test, introduction to notebook structure, and Human Number Line walk. Note: no warm-up this day.
  • Week 2 (Sept 8–12): Expand number line, rational numbers, fraction operations foldable, multiplication/division with multiplicative inverse, skill check on integers and rationals.
  • Week 3 (Sept 15–19): Comparing and ordering challenge, bridge to order of operations, Order of Operations & Evaluating Expressions Scavenger Hunt.
  • Week 4 (Sept 22–26): Properties intro with Equivalent Expressions Matching (discovery task), formal property definitions and practice, simplifying expressions, error analysis.
  • Week 5 (Sept 29–Oct 3): Flexible Family Weekend schedule—analogy brainstorm for "Sell That Property," DeltaMath catch-up, error analysis reinforcement.
  • Week 6 (Oct 6–10): "Sell That Property" poster task, Module 0 Final Challenge (group and individual multi-skill problems), wrap-up.

📦 Module 1: So You Want to Buy a Car

🎯 Overview

Students learn to use algebra as a decision tool through a car‑buying arc: layered percents → spreadsheet fluency → finance & total cost of ownership (TCO) → data displays & evidence‑based comparison → a culminating Car Comparison Project. The sequence deliberately transitions from by‑hand percent reasoning to building an auto‑calculating spreadsheet, then to crafting arguments supported by visuals.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Layered percents: discount → tax/fees → commission; percent error; reasonableness checks.
  • Spreadsheets as calculators: formulas, relative vs. absolute references ($), formatting (%, currency), debugging (#VALUE!, ####), and a “sanity‑check” cell.
  • Finance & TCO: amount financed, simple interest vs. payment idea (PMT), payment schedules, balance graphs, sensitivity (“+1% APR” impact).
  • Data & displays: dot/histogram/box plots; choosing center/spread (mean/median, MAD/IQR) based on shape/skew; reading and comparing distributions.
  • Argumentation: claim–evidence–warrant (CEW) using spreadsheet evidence and visuals; source reliability for real‑world data.

🛠️ Anchor Activities & Milestones

  • Paper Car‑Buying Percent Challenge (Week 7): compute out‑the‑door price by hand; “Bad Deal Detective” traps.
  • IM Unit 1 Section C — Spreadsheets (Week 8): mystery computations → computations → shortcuts; absolute refs; bug‑hunt; add a sanity‑check cell.
  • Conversion Assessment (Week 8 Fri): recreate the paper task as an auto‑calculator (required: one locked ref, one sanity‑check cell, one bug fix).
  • Finance & TCO Extensions (Weeks 9–11): schedule/graph, sensitivity notes, first comparison write‑ups.
  • Mastery Gates: Gate A (Week 10 Fri) — layered % + spreadsheet basics rubric; Gate B (Week 11 Thu) — finance/TCO integrity + interpretation item.
  • Final Car‑Buying Comparison Project (Weeks 12, 14–16): criteria, sources, calculator copy, displays, CEW justification; present in Week 16.

📁 Key Resources

  • Worksheets: Paper Car‑Buying Percent Challenge; “Bad Deal Detective” ad set.
  • Spreadsheets: Paying for a Car template; project copy with Finance/TCO/Displays tabs; rubric with integrity checks.
  • Illustrative Math: Unit 1, Section C (Lessons 6–8) — How to Use Spreadsheets.
  • DeltaMath: Percent change & layered percents; interpreting displays; interest basics (as needed).
  • Writing: CEW sentence frames; source‑reliability mini‑guide.

🔁 Builds On

  • Module 0 number sense, operation order, and expression structure (reasonableness, order matters).
  • Notebook routines and reflection prompts for metacognitive checks.

➡️ Leads To

  • Module 2 functional thinking: tables/graphs, rules from data, and interpretation of patterns.

📅 Weekly Breakdown (aligned to calendar)

  • Week 7 (Oct 13–17) — Launch car‑buying context; criteria brainstorm; classify statistical vs non‑statistical questions; Bad Deal Detective; layered % practice; Paper Percent Challenge A–B. End‑of‑week formative to sort spreadsheet groups.
  • Week 8 (Oct 20–24) — IM Sec C: spreadsheets 101 (cells, =, refs, formats); absolute vs relative references; intentional bug‑fix; add a sanity‑check cell; Conversion Assessment (Fri).
  • Week 9 (Oct 27–31) — Extend calculator: Finance tab v1, amount financed; build payment schedule & graph; TCO tab v1; first display; mini‑write with evidence; sensitivity note (+1% APR impact).
  • Week 10 (Nov 3–7) — Displays & stat choice rule (skew → median/IQR; symmetric → mean/MAD); polish calculator; comparison draft; Mastery Gate A (Fri) with spreadsheet integrity rubric row.
  • Week 11 (Nov 10–14) — Finance v2, scenario switcher; recommendation writing; Mastery Gate B (Thu) (integrity checks + interpretation item); Calculator Challenge submit (Fri).
  • Week 12 (Nov 17–21) — Spiral review; Project Launch: plan, source reliability mini, sheet skeleton; Milestone 0 approval (Fri).
  • Week 13 (Nov 24–28) — Fall Break (no classes).
  • Week 14 (Dec 1–5) — Milestone 1: project copies, initial data, one draft display; mini‑lessons by need (interest nuance, display choice, claim–evidence–warrant).
  • Week 15 (Dec 8–12) — Build week; Milestone 2: data complete, visuals refined, justification in progress; peer feedback.
  • Week 16 (Dec 15–19) — Presentations & reflection; make‑ups/retakes via DeltaMath + office hours.

🧪 Assessments & Evidence

  • Formative: daily warm‑ups, exits, notebook reflections, DeltaMath (M1‑1/2/5), mini‑writes, peer feedback.
  • Checks: Conversion Assessment (Week 8 Fri); Mastery Gate A (Week 10 Fri); Mastery Gate B (Week 11 Thu).
  • Performance: Final Car‑Buying Comparison Project (Weeks 14–16) — rubric includes calculator integrity, visual correctness/readability, and CEW strength.
  • Retakes: Targeted DeltaMath review + office hours to unlock retake; weekly notebook photo submissions via Google Form.

🏅 Skill Points (aligned to Module 1)

  • Earn: on‑time DeltaMath (+1), neat/complete notebook page (+1), evidence‑rich mini‑write (+1), approved project plan (+1), exceptional peer coaching/visuals (+1 each).
  • Spend (practice/challenge only): Check‑One‑Step (1 SP), Hint Card (2 SP), Notes Peek — your notebook, 30s (3 SP). No SP on Conversion Assessment or Mastery Gates.

📦 Module 2: Graphing the Distance

🎯 Overview

Students explore coordinate plane models of real-world and mathematical relationships. Emphasis is placed on graphing, identifying slope and y-intercept, and interpreting what they represent in context.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Graphing from tables and equations
  • Identifying and interpreting slope and intercept
  • Parallel and perpendicular lines (intro)
  • Function notation (introductory)

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Desmos Card Sort: Graph to Story
  • Graphing art extension (optional)

📁 Key Resources

  • DeltaMath: Graphing Lines, Slope
  • Desmos Classroom: Match My Graph

🔁 Builds On

  • Linear contexts from Module 1

➡️ Leads To

  • System of equations
  • Non-linear function comparisons

📅 Weekly Breakdown

  • Week 8 (Oct 21–25): Graphing from tables and equations
  • Week 9 (Oct 28–Nov 1): Identifying slope and intercept from graphs
  • Week 10 (Nov 4–8): Function notation and intro to systems

📦 Module 3: Functions and Relationships

🎯 Overview

This module focuses on building a formal understanding of functions. Students will define, evaluate, and compare functions using tables, graphs, and verbal descriptions. The concept of a function as a rule assigning each input exactly one output is central.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Definition of a function
  • Function notation and evaluation
  • Comparing functions represented in different forms
  • Interpreting domain and range in context

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Function Machines & input-output mapping
  • Desmos: Function Carnival
  • Function Detective gallery walk

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Unit 4
  • Desmos Activities on Function Structure

🔁 Builds On

  • Graphing and context modeling from Module 2

➡️ Leads To

  • Systems of equations and non-linear comparisons

📅 Weekly Breakdown

  • Week 11 (Nov 11–15): What is a function? Exploring representations
  • Week 12 (Nov 18–22): Function notation and evaluations
  • Week 13 (Nov 25–27): Short week – Comparing functions and real-world applications

📦 Module 4: Life Lines (Semester Close)

🎯 Overview

This culminating mini-module invites students to reflect on their semester learning by applying linear models to their own lives. Students create a “Life Line” project that connects math to personal growth, experiences, or goals using the tools of linear modeling.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Reinforcing slope-intercept form
  • Interpreting intercepts and slope in context
  • Storytelling through data

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Peer gallery walk & feedback session

📁 Key Resources

🔁 Builds On

➡️ Leads To

  • Semester reflection and next-term goal setting

📅 Weekly Breakdown

  • Week 15 (Dec 9–13): Draft and complete visuals, begin reflection writing
  • Week 16 (Dec 16–20): Finalize project, peer feedback, semester close
