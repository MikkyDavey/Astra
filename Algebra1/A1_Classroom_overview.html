Folder System: Manila vs Notebook Pockets

  • Manila folders: Used for distributing handouts, visually identifying seating on change days, and storing unfinished work for the week. Any work that is not yet completed or submitted by the end of the week remains in the manila folder until it is finished, turned in, or added to the student's notebook.
  • Notebook divider folders (pockets): Used for storing finished or returned work for the current module. Students fold sheets and keep them in the appropriate notebook pocket for easy access and organization.
  • Manila folders are checked weekly on Mondays to ensure students are clearing out completed assignments and organizing their materials properly.
  • Monday folder checks are tied to seating shuffle routines: folders are placed on tables at the start of the week, doubling as visual seating identifiers. This makes the check feel like part of the normal Monday setup and ensures consistent weekly organization.

Materials & Transitions

  • Ready to start: Notebook, pencil, calculator (if needed), warm-up slip taped, device asleep unless asked.
  • Handouts: Pick up from front bins; tape into notebook immediately; extras kept in a labeled folder.
  • Devices: Used when prompted for DeltaMath/Desmos; put away during direct instruction and group modeling.
  • Transitions: Use visual timer/emoji poll on Classroom Screen; groups earn SP for fast, quiet resets.
