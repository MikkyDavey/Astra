Week 4: Order of Operations & Bridging Concepts

  • Monday:
    • Routine: Monday Manila Folder Check — place folders at tables; quick scan for unfinished/returned work; mark missing items in gradebook notes.
    • Spiral Review Warm-Up: brief revisit of 1–2 key ideas from the prior week to strengthen retrieval.
    • 5 min – Warm-up: quick number magnitude estimation (mixed forms)
    • 25 min – Group Challenge: Compare & Order Across Forms (integers, fractions, decimals, scientific notation, roots, π) – review strategies from Week 3
    • 10 min – Debrief: connect magnitude and form to operation order reasoning
    • 10 min – Notebook reflection: “How does knowing which numbers are bigger help me decide which calculations to do first?”
    • Ambiguous Ranking – with Hidden Conventions
      Groups are given 5–6 expressions designed to be easy to misinterpret:
      • Division/multiplication tie: 8 ÷ 2 × 4
      • Negative sign with powers: -3^2 vs. (-3)^2
      • Fraction bar precedence: 10 – 4 / 2 and 3 / 1/2
      • Roots with multiplication: √4 × 9 vs. √(4 × 9)
      • Implied multiplication vs. explicit: 1/2x vs. (1/2)·x
      Groups rank the list from smallest to largest, then compare with a different group who got a different order and debate which conventions are correct. Leave some disputes unresolved as a teaser for Tuesday’s formal discussion of order of operations.
  • Tuesday:
    • 5 min – Warm-up: simple mixed operations without parentheses
    • 15 min – Mini-lesson: Order of Operations (PEMDAS as a hierarchy, not just a list); connect to real number system work
    • 20 min – Guided practice: mixed operations and return to Monday's ambiguous hierarchy (integers, fractions, decimals)
    • 12 min – Mastery Gate 0C: Order of Operations (numeric) & Evaluating simple expressions — short in‑class gate to confirm readiness for Thursday variables and Friday scavenger hunt.
    • 8 min – Start DeltaMath #3: Order of Operations & Evaluating Expressions (due Friday); students below threshold get an auto‑assigned review + office hours invite.
    • Reflection: “Write one expression that could be misread and explain how you’d make it unambiguous.”
  • Thursday:
    • 5 min – Warm-up: If x = 3, which is greater: 2x + 5 or 2(x + 5)?
    • 15 min – Mini-lesson: Introducing variables as placeholders for numbers. Explain that variables are symbols (like x) that can stand for different values, and that they allow us to write rules and expressions that work for many numbers. Demonstrate evaluating expressions by substituting specific values for variables and show that variables follow the same arithmetic rules as numbers. Emphasize that sometimes expressions with variables cannot be simplified further unless we know the value of the variable.
    • 15 min – Practice: Evaluate expressions with given variable values and identify expressions that cannot be simplified without knowing the variable. Reinforce the idea that variables behave like numbers in arithmetic operations.
    • 10 min – Work time: continue DeltaMath #3 Order of Operations and practice evaluating expressions with variables and numbers.
    • 5 min – Exit check/reflection: How is evaluating an expression with a variable similar to and different from working with plain numbers?
  • Friday:
    • 5 min – Introduction: Explain the Order of Operations & Evaluating Expressions Scavenger Hunt. Review expectations, materials, and how to rotate through stations. Highlight how this activity will help prepare for future skills and assessments.
    • 40 min – Scavenger Hunt Stations: Students rotate through a series of stations, each featuring a different problem or puzzle related to order of operations and evaluating expressions (with and without variables). Encourage collaboration and discussion at each station. Teacher circulates to support and check for understanding.
    • 5 min – Wrap-up/Reflection: Gather students to share strategies, discuss the most challenging problems, and highlight key takeaways. Prompt reflection: “What’s one thing you’ll watch for when simplifying expressions?” Remind students to check that all their work is recorded in their notebooks.
    • SP options (Challenge): Hint Card (2 SP), Notes Peek – your own notebook, 30s (3 SP), Check‑One‑Step (1 SP). Optionally, fastest accurate path earns table +2 SP.
    • Absent students or those needing more time: May continue the scavenger hunt during office hours on Monday or Tuesday of Week 5; final due date for completion is Wednesday of Week 5.
    • Improving scores: Students may improve their Skill Check scores by completing a DeltaMath review assignment, then scheduling a retake after the review is complete.
    • 10 min – Logistics: DeltaMath due (10 PM), submit Weekly Notebook Check (Google Form), preview Week 5 (properties & simplifying expressions)
  • Additional Notes:
    • Comparing & Ordering task serves as a conceptual bridge from magnitude sense to operation reasoning.
    • Highlight the connection between number sense and correct order of operations.
    • Use examples with both known values and variable placeholders to build flexibility.
    • Reinforce notebook use: all warm-ups and reflections should be glued or written in.
    • EARN: on‑time DeltaMath (+1), collaborative norms observed (+1).
    • Table Award Theme: Most Respectful Disagreement in Ambiguous Ranking (+2 SP).

Week 5: Properties of Real Numbers – Family Weekend Mode

  • Monday:
    • Routine: Monday Manila Folder Check — place folders at tables; quick scan for unfinished/returned work; mark missing items in gradebook notes.
    • Spiral Review Warm-Up: brief revisit of 1–2 key ideas from the prior week to strengthen retrieval.
    • 5 min – Warm-up: “same value” teaser: Show two different expressions equal to the same thing (e.g., 3(x+4) and 3x+12) and ask “Why are these equal?” Collect a few student guesses without naming properties.
    • 20–25 min – Discovery Task: Matching Equivalent Expressions — students get a set of expression cards; find matches and write in their own words why the match works. (No property names yet.) (Uses the expression-only half of a master card sort set; students will re-use this set with added property names/definitions on Tuesday.)
    • 10 min – Whole-class share: groups present matches and reasoning; teacher records student language for later linking to formal property names.
    • 5 min – Closure: Reflection prompt: “What’s one thing you noticed about how expressions can change but keep the same value?” Tape into notebook for later revisiting.
  • Tuesday:
    • 5 min – Warm-up (bridge to Monday): Project 2 pairs from Monday’s set. Prompt on slide: “How do you know these pairs are the same value? Describe the move in your own words (no names yet).” (Pair A: 3(x+4) → 3x+12; Pair B: (4·2)·6 → 4·(2·6))
    • 10 min – Name the moves: introduce commutative, associative, distributive, identity (0 and 1), and inverse properties with concise examples that match Monday’s puzzles.
    • Post Success Criteria visibly: what mastery of each property looks like, with examples.
    • 10 min – Card Sort: Students use the full card sort set (expressions plus property names/definitions) to match each “move” to its property. Groups discuss and justify matches, using Monday’s language and new property names. Teacher circulates to prompt and clarify. Require a one‑line justification per match. Keep property names visible in a word bank for reference.
    • 15 min – Guided → Independent Practice with Group Support: Use the provided property practice sheet. In groups, each member must choose a different example if possible, complete it, then explain their reasoning aloud to the group. Rotate until all have shared. Teacher circulates to check accuracy and understanding. On the practice sheet, include a margin column for “Property” and “Because…” so students must pair the name with their reason.
    • 5 min – Bonus/extension: Groups create one original example for a property not in their set, swap with another group, and identify each other’s property.
    • Reflection: “When would naming the property help you communicate or check your work?”
    • 5 min – Logistics: launch DeltaMath #4 (Properties & Simplifying) — short set; open through next Friday.
  • Thursday (Family Weekend visitors likely):
    • 5–7 min – Warm-Up: “Spot the mistake” problem on board; pairs discuss and write which property was violated.
    • 10 min – Mini-Lesson: Model 2–3 step simplifications, pausing for students to name the property after each step. Include distributive + combining like terms + identity/inverse examples.
    • 15–18 min – Main Activity: Visitor-friendly paired challenge — solve 2–3 multi-step problems, labeling each step with the property name and a short “because” statement. Encourage color-coding property names.
    • 10 min – Error Analysis: Use simplification error analysis set. Small groups identify and correct each error, explaining which property misuse caused it.
    • Skill Points: During paired challenge/error analysis: Hint Card (2 SP) and Check‑One‑Step (1 SP) available.
    • 5 min – Wrap-Up/Reflection: “Which property did you use most today? Which one tripped you up?”
  • Friday (Family Weekend, possible altered schedule):
    • 5–7 min – Warm-Up: Error Analysis Mini-Set — 2–3 short simplification problems with deliberate mistakes. Students identify the error, name the violated property, and fix it.
    • 3–5 min – Mini-Intro to “Sell That Property” (Week 6 project): Explain that students will become “salespeople” for a math property next week. Show 1–2 fun analogy examples (e.g., Distributive = “the buy-one-get-one of math,” Identity = “an invisibility cloak for numbers”).
    • 15–20 min – Brainstorm Challenge: Students choose one property they like or understand best. Create a draft analogy, metaphor, slogan, or skit idea for that property. They can write, doodle, or sketch their idea.
    • 5–10 min – Quick Share-Out: A few volunteers share their analogy/slogan. Class discusses what made them memorable. Teacher records standout ideas to bring into Week 6.
    • 10–15 min – Housekeeping / Work Time: Complete DeltaMath #4, submit Weekly Notebook Check (Google Form), organize handouts, or catch up on missing work.
    • Skill Points: EARN +1 SP for DeltaMath catch‑up, +1 SP for notebook polish stamp. Light‑stakes SP week due to Family Weekend.
    • Reflection: “If you had to ‘sell’ one math property today, which would you pick and what’s your opening line?”
  • Additional Notes:
    • Homework and assessments lighter this week to accommodate Family Weekend schedules.
    • Encourage verbal explanations and "because" statements; value the explanation over the label early in the week.
    • Build informal familiarity before formal naming (Mon → Tue).
    • Thread properties into earlier contexts (integers, rationals, fraction operations) to avoid feeling abstract.
    • Include reflexive, symmetric, transitive in equation-justification puzzles so students see them as reasoning tools, not trivia.
    • Keep groups stable through Week 5; plan reshuffle for Week 7 (new groups begin Monday of Week 7).
    • Table Award Theme: Sharpest Property Justification (+2 SP).
