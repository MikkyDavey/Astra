Week 1 (Sept 1–5) — First Breath: Definitions & Tools

Focus: IM U1.L1–L2 • Launch precise definitions & tool fluency; set classroom systems. Day 1 mirrors Algebra 1 welcome flow.

Daily Plan (50 min each)

  • Mon — First Day: Welcome & Norms
    • 5 min – Welcome and settle in
    • 10 min – Folder handout and math interest survey
    • 15 min – One-page placement test with quiet music
    • 20 min – Folder coloring, stickers, and optional coloring pages
    • Reflection: “What part of today made this class feel doable for you? What will help you focus here?”
  • Tue — IM U1 Lesson 1: 1.1 The Right Tool + 1.2 Illegal Construction Moves
    • 5 min – Folder‑based seating and welcome back
    • 10 min – Syllabus and classroom norms overview
    • 10 min – Introduce group task structure + Skill Points (how to earn this week)
    • 10 min – Activity: IM U1 L1.1 “The Right Tool” (quick tool choice exploration; keep notation minimal—points only as needed)
    • 20 min – Activity: IM U1 L1.2 “Illegal Construction Moves” (test moves, decide legal/illegal, record brief notes)
    • Reflection: “What makes this construction more precise than the sketch you made in the Warm‑up?”
    • SP: on‑task participation • helpful peer coaching • neat notebook setup
  • Thu — IM U1 Lesson 1: 1.3 Can You Make a Perfect Copy? (Hexagon)
    • Warm‑Up Handout (at entry) — Students complete the pencil‑only hexagon sketch on the color‑coded warm‑up/reflection sheet (tape Warm‑Up at start); clip the Reflection prompt to the page for later use; keep sheet in folder until notebook time.
    • 5 min – IM 1.3 Launch: Legal vs. illegal moves (recap from Tue); record in IM handout (not in notebook yet).
    • 10 min – Independent construction attempt: Build a regular hexagon using only legal moves (compass + straightedge). SP: accuracy • productive struggle
    • 5 min – Teacher demo & discussion: Model a perfect hexagon; how to detect precision vs. inaccuracy (use point/line/plane vocabulary naturally).
    • 15 min – Notebook Launch: Expectations & procedure; create the Module 1 mini‑folder; decorate covers; add reinforcements/tabs.
    • 15 min – First Notebook Entries: Tape in today’s warm‑up sheet and the final hexagon attempt side‑by‑side; write the first reflection on the facing page.
    • SP: organized setup • neat tape‑ins • clear reflection comparing sketch vs. construction
  • Fri — Discovering Geometry: Building Blocks of Geometry (Point • Line • Plane)
    • 5 min – Warm‑Up (image set): Find examples/non‑examples of point, line, and plane in photos/diagrams
    • 7 min – Mini context: Why these are "undefined" terms; ancient attempts at definitions (short quotes)
    • 8 min – Individual write: Draft your own definitions for point, line, plane
    • 12 min – Small‑group protocol: Read each person’s definitions aloud, explain, question, then revise together to a group definition
    • 10 min – Whole‑class “balloon up”: Groups propose their best wording; converge on concise class definitions; record on board
    • 5 min – Notebook tape‑in: Create the first vocabulary pages (point • line • plane) with the class consensus + one non‑example each
    • Unclip & complete today’s Reflection prompt on the taped handout page: “What makes a definition ‘good’? Which of today’s was hardest to pin down and why?”
    • SP: clear mathematical language • example/non‑example justification • collaborative listening • tidy handout management (tape/clip)

Notebook Inserts: Weekly warm‑up/reflection handout (color‑coded) • Legal/Illegal Moves mini‑list • Hexagon attempt tape‑in • Vocabulary pages: point/line/plane

Resources: IM Geometry U1.L1 full (1.1–1.3) • Discovering Geometry “Building Blocks of Geometry” (undefined terms) • All Things Algebra vocab cards (optional)

Next Steps: Print bisector step cards; prep GeoGebra file for locus trace; queue Desmos Transformations Golf for Week 4. Generate daily handouts (Mon–Fri) with titles, Warm‑Ups, and clipped Reflections for Weeks 1–2.

Week 2 (Sept 8–12) — Constructing Patterns + Perpendicular Bisectors

Focus: IM U1.L2–L3 • Precision in written instructions; vocabulary for lines (parallel, perpendicular) and perpendicular bisector as an equidistance locus; early reasoning/proof language (“because…” stems).

Daily Plan (50 min each)

  • Mon — IM U1.L2: Constructing Patterns
    • 5 min – Warm‑Up: Math Talk prompt on radii/equidistance (L2.1) — hand signal routine
    • 8 min – Mini: What makes instructions precise? Labels, defined moves, orientation cues
    • 20 min – Activity 2.2 “Make Your Own”: Create a pattern from a circle+radius; record every step with precise language
    • 12 min – Activity 2.3 “Make Someone Else’s”: Trade & recreate; highlight clear vs. ambiguous wording
    • 5 min – Cool‑down 2.4: Quick revisions to instructions (save in mini‑folder)
    • SP: precision in language • complete labeling • thoughtful revision
  • Tue — IM U1.L2 wrap + IM U1.L3 Launch
    • 5 min – Warm‑Up: Identify parallel vs. “seems parallel” in a student‑made pattern (ties to L2 parallel‑line construction)
    • 10 min – Mini: Read/perform IM’s step‑by‑step parallel‑line construction (from 2.3); add to “Legal Moves” list
    • 15 min – Activity 3.1 “Find All the Points!”: Circles as sets of equidistant points; mark intersections
    • 15 min – Activity 3.2 “Human Perpendicular Bisector”: Class physically finds the equidistance set; capture conjecture
    • 5 min – Reflection (tape‑in): “Our conjecture says ______. What evidence did we produce?”
    • SP: participation in human model • clear diagram capturing the conjecture line
  • Thu — IM U1.L3: How Well Can You Slice It?
    • 5 min – Warm‑Up: Diagnose a “not a perpendicular bisector” example (angle vs. midpoint error)
    • 10 min – Mini: Definitions & notation: ⟂, right angle marker, congruent segment ticks; “because…” stems
    • 20 min – Activity 3.3: Construct the perpendicular bisector by compass/straightedge; optionally compare to paper‑fold
    • 10 min – Notebook tape‑ins: final construction with 2–3 justification sentences; add to “Legal/Illegal Moves” page
    • 5 min – Reflection: “How does the intersecting‑circles method guarantee both perpendicular and midpoint?”
    • SP: accurate construction • justification quality • tidy tape‑ins
  • Fri — Skill Gate SG1 + Targeted Practice
    • 8 min – Warm‑Up: Quick ID — which instructions would reproduce the pattern? (choose best of 3)
    • 12 min – Skill Gate SG1: Construct a perpendicular bisector from a fresh segment and write 2 “because…” statements
    • 20 min – Targeted practice: IM practice set + All Things Algebra items (parallel line via construction; bisector fluency)
    • 5 min – Notebook check stamps (peer‑assisted) & SP nominations via Google Form (due tonight)
    • 5 min – Reflection: “One wording change that makes my instructions unambiguous is…”

Notebook Inserts: “Make Your Own” instruction sheet (original + revised) • Parallel‑line construction steps (from L2.3) • Perpendicular bisector exemplar with reasons • “Because…” stem bank.

Resources: IM G U1.L2–L3; GeoGebra Constructions Tool; All Things Algebra targeted practice.

Next Steps: Prep ∥/⟂‑through‑a‑point guides for Week 3; print Square‑in‑Circle template; queue short gallery of strong instruction rewrites for Monday SP stamps.
