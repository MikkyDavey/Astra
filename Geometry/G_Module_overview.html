üì¶ Module 1: From Definitions to Design

üéØ Overview

Module 1 is structured as a progression from routines and tool introduction, to diagram and instruction literacy, then vocabulary expansion and definition writing, followed by angle measurement and relationships, and finally constructions consolidation and midpoint review.

Week 1: Students set up folders and notebooks, learn seating and classroom routines, and are introduced to Skill Gates. DeltaMath is introduced with due dates noted in class rather than daily. Students engage in discovery-first diagram exploration and matching, and practice giving and following step-by-step instructions. Formal definitions for point, line, and plane are taped into vocabulary dictionaries. SP routines and a Friday group scoreboard celebration are established.

Week 2: Vocabulary is expanded (ray, line segment, endpoints, coplanar, collinear, intersection, congruent, parallel, perpendicular, bisector, circle, center, radius), with Flashcard Factory after each major vocab build. Students analyze and write precise instructions for constructions, practice diagram interpretation and labeling, and participate in ‚ÄúWhat makes a good definition?‚Äù activities using unsigned notecards. ‚ÄúMake Your Own‚Äù and ‚ÄúMatch Mine‚Äù tasks are used to foster precision and collaboration.

Week 3: Focus shifts to angle measurement, naming conventions, and angle relationships (vertical angles, parallel lines), with algebraic puzzles for practice. Students begin hands-on constructions: equilateral triangle (Friday anchor) and perpendicular bisector. Definition-writing circuits and diagram labeling continue to build reasoning and vocabulary.

Week 4: Students consolidate construction skills (perpendicular bisector, angle bisector), connect coordinate midpoint concepts using Math Medic before IM 1.6 stations, and review all skills in preparation for the performance assessment. Anchor activities include IM 1.6 Constructions Stations and Friday group SP awards.

  • Week 1: Routines, folders, seating, Skill Gates, DeltaMath intro (due dates in notes), discovery-first diagram exploration and matching, formal definitions (point, line, plane), SP routines, Friday group scoreboard celebration.
  • Week 2: Expanded vocabulary, Flashcard Factory after vocab builds, instruction writing for constructions, ‚ÄúWhat makes a good definition?‚Äù with unsigned note cards, ‚ÄúMake Your Own‚Äù and ‚ÄúMatch Mine‚Äù tasks.
  • Week 3: Angle measurement/naming, angle relationships (vertical, parallel lines), algebraic puzzles, equilateral triangle construction (Friday anchor), definition-writing circuit (Thursday), ongoing diagram literacy.
  • Week 4: Constructions consolidation (perpendicular bisector, angle bisector), coordinate midpoint connection (Math Medic), IM 1.6 stations, review for performance, Friday SP group awards.

üì¶ Module 2: Proving Triangles

üéØ Overview

Students develop congruence reasoning and learn to communicate arguments using flexible proof formats. Focus areas include SSS, SAS, ASA/AAS (HL as time allows), careful diagram reading (no unwarranted assumptions), and building proof maps (flowcharts, narratives, annotated diagrams). Anchor activities and Skill Gates are sequenced to support proof fluency, with buffer days for practice and remediation before break.

  • Initial lessons: diagram reading, marking correspondence, proof vocabulary; scaffold proof formats gradually.
  • Skill Gates and DeltaMath review for proof concepts; anchor activities scheduled mid-module.
  • Buffer days for remediation, extra practice, and check-ins before break.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Triangle congruence criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS (HL as time allows)
  • Marking and naming correspondence for clarity
  • Proof formats: flowchart, paragraph, annotated-diagram
  • Auxiliary constructions to unlock proofs and connect to coordinate geometry

üõ†Ô∏è Anchor Activities

  • Congruence card sorts and error analysis (early module; conceptual clarity)
  • Explore-your-own proof diagrams (student-designed logic maps; formative assessment)
  • Wild Wonders Coordinate Connection (midpoint & distance; congruence application)
  • Triangle stability/design justification applied task (end module; synthesis)

üìÅ Key Resources

üì¶ Module 3: Symmetry & Similarity

üéØ Overview

Symmetry is explored through transformations, then scaled reasoning develops into formal similarity. The module is often split across the semester break: Part 1 (transformations and symmetry), Part 2 (similarity and proportional reasoning). Students practice triangle similarity criteria, proportional reasoning, and indirect measurement. Desmos Transformations Golf anchors transformation fluency; indirect measurement poster is the applied capstone.

  • Part 1: Transformations and symmetry (pre-break); Part 2: Similarity and proportional reasoning (post-break).
  • Desmos activities coordinated with tech access; poster project at end of Part 2.
  • SP and DeltaMath reviews aligned with similarity criteria and proportional reasoning.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Lines/rotations of symmetry; compositions of rigid motions
  • Dilations and similarity: AA, SSS~, SAS~ criteria
  • Proportional reasoning with side ratios and parallel lines
  • Indirect measurement (mirror method), coordinate connections

üõ†Ô∏è Anchor Activities

  • Similarity labs with proportional reasoning puzzles (mid Part 2; scaffolded tasks)
  • Indirect Measurement Poster Project (mirror method; capstone, 1‚Äì2 class periods)
  • Quadrilateral hierarchy & parallelogram properties (optional; notebook inserts)

üì¶ Module 4: Trigonometry in Action

üéØ Overview

Students ground sine, cosine, and tangent in similar right triangles, then apply trig to solve missing sides/angles and model real contexts. The Miracle on the Hudson task and the Trig in Action poster project serve as central capstones. Pacing includes review days and buffers for absences.

  • Trig introduced after similarity; review days built in.
  • Miracle on the Hudson (mid-module); poster project at end.
  • SP routines and DeltaMath review for trig vocabulary and problems.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Right triangle similarity, ratio tables
  • SOH-CAH-TOA, inverse trig for angle finding
  • Angles of elevation/depression, modeling real problems
  • Special right triangles (45-45-90, 30-60-90)

üì¶ Module 5: Circles in Motion

üéØ Overview

Circles become a conceptual and symbolic capstone, synthesizing constructions, proof habits, similarity, and trigonometry. Students investigate chords, arcs, central/inscribed angles, tangent lines, and radian measure. Connections to right triangles and similarity are explicit; understanding is demonstrated through problem solving and the culminating Circle Design Challenge.

  • Circle vocabulary/theorems early; scaffold chord/tangent investigations.
  • Labs on radians, arc/sector calculations with manipulatives/technology.
  • Circle Design Challenge: final project; allow multiple sessions for design/presentation.
  • SP and DeltaMath review cycles reinforce key concepts.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Circle vocabulary; central vs. inscribed angles; arc measures
  • Tangent-line theorems; chord properties; cyclic quadrilaterals (select)
  • Radians, arc length, sector area
  • Right-triangle structures within circles

  • Radians with manipulatives; arc/sector calculation labs (mid-late module; hands-on)
