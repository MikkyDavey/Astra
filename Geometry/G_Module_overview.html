📘 Geometry Module Overview

📦 Module 1: From Definitions to Design

🎯 Overview

Students launch Geometry with Week 1 routines including folder creation, seating arrangements, classroom norms, and an introduction to Skill Gates (SP) focused on accuracy, persistence, and collaboration. Notebook setup follows the folder organization to support learning. Daily SP opportunities are embedded, with warm-up and reflection handouts introduced starting Thursday. The procedural flow aligns with classroom rhythm: warm-ups begin each session, reflections are taped in after activities, and Skill Gates are integrated throughout. Students build precise language and tool fluency (compass, straightedge, dynamic geometry), learning how definitions drive construction, how markings carry meaning, and how careful diagramming supports later proof. The module closes with a 3D Printing Design Challenge that synthesizes construction skills, transformation intuition, and measurement for print-ready design.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Precise definitions, labeling, and diagram conventions
  • Compass & straightedge basics: circles, perpendicular/angle bisectors
  • Equilateral triangles; parallel/perpendicular lines by construction
  • Intro rigid transformations: translations, reflections, rotations
  • Notebook + journal routines (inserts, reflection prompts)

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Constructions Lab: equilateral triangle, perpendicular bisector, angle bisector
  • Desmos Geometry samplers for rigid motion intuition
  • "What makes a good definition?" card sort + rewrite
  • Mini design task: symmetric tile or logo (paper or digital)
  • Illustrative Math Lessons 1.1–1.3 structure with undefined terms investigation on Friday

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Unit 1: Constructions & Rigid Transformations
  • Math Medic: Intro to Geometry / Reasoning openers
  • All Things Algebra: Vocabulary & diagram practice
  • Notebook inserts: Tools reference, markings guide, construction steps

🔁 Builds On

  • Middle school vocabulary and basic angle/segment ideas
  • Growth from Algebra 1 notebooks to Geometry notebook + journal

➡️ Leads To

  • Module 2 proof habits (flowcharts, visual logic)
  • Module 3 transformations & similarity groundwork

📦 Module 2: Proving Triangles

🎯 Overview

Students develop congruence reasoning and learn to communicate arguments using flexible proof formats. Emphasis is on SSS, SAS, ASA/AAS (with HL as time allows), reading diagrams without assumption, and building proof maps (flowchart, narrative, annotated diagrams). This reasoning leads directly into later similarity and trigonometry work. Pacing is planned to protect time for proof practice and recovery before break.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Triangle congruence criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS (HL optional)
  • Marking correspondence and naming for clarity
  • Proof formats: flowchart, paragraph, annotated-diagram proof
  • Auxiliary constructions to unlock proofs

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Congruence card sorts and error analysis
  • Explore-your-own proof diagrams (student-designed logic maps)
  • Wild Wonders Coordinate Connection (midpoint & distance links)
  • Applied task: triangle stability / design justification

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Unit 2: Congruence
  • Math Medic: Triangle Proofs Unit
  • All Things Algebra: Proof puzzles & practice
  • Notebook inserts: Congruence reference, proof flowchart template

🔁 Builds On

  • Module 1 definitions, constructions, and transformation intuition

➡️ Leads To

  • Module 3 similarity proofs; Module 4 trig via similar triangles

📦 Module 3: Symmetry & Similarity

🎯 Overview

Symmetry is explored through transformations, then scaled reasoning develops into formal similarity. This module is often split across the semester break into Part 1 (transformations and symmetry) and Part 2 (similarity and proportional reasoning). Students practice proportional reasoning, triangle similarity criteria, and indirect measurement. The Desmos Transformations Golf anchors transformation fluency, and the indirect measurement mini-poster serves as the applied capstone.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Lines/rotations of symmetry; compositions of rigid motions
  • Dilations and similarity; AA, SSS~, SAS~ criteria
  • Proportional reasoning with side ratios and parallel lines
  • Indirect measurement (mirror method) and coordinate links

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Desmos Transformations Golf (Activity Builder)
  • Similarity labs with proportional reasoning puzzles
  • Indirect Measurement Poster Project (mirror method)
  • Quadrilateral hierarchy & parallelogram properties (selective)

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Units 1 & 3: Transformations & Similarity
  • Math Medic: Transformations & Similarity tasks
  • All Things Algebra: Proportion practice sets
  • Notebook inserts: Similarity ratio ladder, transformation organizer

🔁 Builds On

  • Modules 1–2: precise diagrams, congruence logic

➡️ Leads To

  • Module 4 trigonometry (ratios in right triangles)
  • Module 5 circle theorems via similar/inscribed triangles

📦 Module 4: Trigonometry in Action

🎯 Overview

Students ground sine, cosine, and tangent in similar right triangles, then apply trig to solve missing sides/angles and model real contexts. The Miracle on the Hudson task and the Trig in Action poster project serve as the central capstone. Pacing accounts for February illness/absence buffers.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Right triangle similarity and ratio tables
  • SOH-CAH-TOA, inverse trig for angle finding
  • Angles of elevation/depression; problem modeling
  • Special right triangles (45-45-90, 30-60-90)

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Math Medic: Special Right Triangles; Intro to Trig; Applications
  • "Miracle on the Hudson" contextual task
  • Trig in Action Poster Project (optional presentation for skill points)

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Unit 4: Right Triangle Trigonometry
  • Math Medic: Trigonometry Unit
  • All Things Algebra: Reinforcement practice as needed
  • Notebook inserts: Trig tables, special triangles reference

🔁 Builds On

  • Module 3 similarity and proportional reasoning

➡️ Leads To

  • Module 5 circle connections (tangents, radians, sectors)

📦 Module 5: Circles in Motion

🎯 Overview

Circles become a conceptual and symbolic capstone for the course, drawing together constructions, proof habits, similarity, and trigonometry. Students investigate chords, arcs, central/inscribed angles, tangent lines, and the power of radian measure. Connections to right triangles and similarity are made explicit, and students demonstrate understanding through problem solving and design. The Circle Design Challenge is the culminating artifact.

📚 Skills & Topics

  • Circle vocabulary; central vs. inscribed angles; arc measures
  • Tangent-line theorems; chord properties; cyclic quadrilaterals (select)
  • Radians, arc length, and sector area
  • Right-triangle structures hidden in circles

🛠️ Anchor Activities

  • Mystery Measurement diagram challenge (Math Medic)
  • Tangent & chord theorem investigations (visual proofs)
  • Radians with manipulatives; arc/sector calculation labs
  • Circle Design Challenge (poster or digital)

📁 Key Resources

  • Illustrative Math Unit 7: Circles
  • Math Medic: Circles Unit
  • All Things Algebra: Targeted practice sets
  • Notebook inserts: Circle theorems flipbook; radian wheel

🔁 Builds On

  • Modules 1–4: constructions, proof habits, similarity, trigonometry

➡️ Leads To

  • Synthesis & Celebration (spiral review, public display)
  • Preparation for Algebra 2 connections (unit circle preview)
