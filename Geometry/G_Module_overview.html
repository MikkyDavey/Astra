üì¶ Module 1: From Definitions to Design

üéØ Overview

The first weeks focus on establishing classroom systems, tool fluency, and collaborative norms while working discovery-first. Students launch Geometry with Week 1 routines including folder creation, seating arrangements, classroom norms, and an introduction to Skill Gates (Skill Gates ‚Äì checks that students have the skills they need to continue; marks that those skills will be expected from then on), which are paired with automated Deltamath review and section retakes by Wednesday EOD. Warm-ups and reflections are combined on a single color-coded sheet starting Thursday of Week 1. This class often begins with discovery-based visual work rather than a mini-lesson first; students frequently explore and make sense of diagrams before formal definitions are taped into notebooks. In Week 1, students tape formal definitions for point, line, and plane into their vocabulary dictionary. Notebook setup follows the folder organization to support learning. Daily SP opportunities are embedded, with warm-up and reflection handouts introduced starting Thursday. The procedural flow aligns with classroom rhythm: warm-ups begin each session, reflections are taped in after activities, and Skill Gates are integrated throughout. DeltaMath is introduced early for both class Skill Gates and individualized review assignments generated from results. On Friday of Week 2, students add vocabulary terms including ray, line segment, endpoints, coplanar, collinear, intersection, congruent, parallel, perpendicular, bisector, circle (includes center), and radius. Students build precise language and tool fluency (compass, straightedge, dynamic geometry), learning how definitions drive construction, how markings carry meaning, and how careful diagramming supports later proof. Angle naming and measurement basics are introduced in Week 3. The module closes with a 3D Printing Design Challenge that synthesizes construction skills, transformation intuition, and measurement for print-ready design.

üì¶ Module 2: Proving Triangles

üéØ Overview

Students develop congruence reasoning and learn to communicate arguments using flexible proof formats. Emphasis is on SSS, SAS, ASA/AAS (with HL as time allows), reading diagrams without assumption, and building proof maps (flowchart, narrative, annotated diagrams). This reasoning leads directly into later similarity and trigonometry work. Pacing is planned to protect time for proof practice and recovery before break.

  • (Plan initial lessons to focus on diagram reading and assumptions; scaffold proof formats gradually)
  • (Integrate Skill Gates and DM review for proof vocabulary and concepts; schedule anchor activities mid-module)
  • (Allow buffer days before break for remediation and practice)

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Triangle congruence criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS (HL optional)
  • Marking correspondence and naming for clarity
  • Proof formats: flowchart, paragraph, annotated-diagram proof
  • Auxiliary constructions to unlock proofs

üõ†Ô∏è Anchor Activities

  • Explore-your-own proof diagrams (student-designed logic maps) (mid-module; formative assessment)
  • Wild Wonders Coordinate Connection (midpoint & distance links) (connects coordinate geometry with congruence)
  • Applied task: triangle stability / design justification (end Module 2; synthesis task)

üì¶ Module 3: Symmetry & Similarity

üéØ Overview

Symmetry is explored through transformations, then scaled reasoning develops into formal similarity. This module is often split across the semester break into Part 1 (transformations and symmetry) and Part 2 (similarity and proportional reasoning). Students practice proportional reasoning, triangle similarity criteria, and indirect measurement. The Desmos Transformations Golf anchors transformation fluency, and the indirect measurement mini-poster serves as the applied capstone.

  • (Plan Part 1 before break focusing on transformations and symmetry; Part 2 after break on similarity and proportional reasoning)
  • (Coordinate Desmos activities with tech access; schedule poster project near end of Part 2)
  • (Embed SP and DM reviews aligned with similarity criteria)

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Lines/rotations of symmetry; compositions of rigid motions
  • Dilations and similarity; AA, SSS~, SAS~ criteria
  • Proportional reasoning with side ratios and parallel lines
  • Indirect measurement (mirror method) and coordinate links

üì¶ Module 4: Trigonometry in Action

üéØ Overview

Students ground sine, cosine, and tangent in similar right triangles, then apply trig to solve missing sides/angles and model real contexts. The Miracle on the Hudson task and the Trig in Action poster project serve as the central capstone. Pacing accounts for February illness/absence buffers.

  • (Plan trig introduction after similarity foundation; pace to allow for review days)
  • (Schedule Miracle on the Hudson task mid-module; poster project near end)
  • (Embed SP routines and DM review for trig vocabulary and problem types)

üìö Skills & Topics

üì¶ Module 5: Circles in Motion

üéØ Overview

Circles become a conceptual and symbolic capstone for the course, drawing together constructions, proof habits, similarity, and trigonometry. Students investigate chords, arcs, central/inscribed angles, tangent lines, and the power of radian measure. Connections to right triangles and similarity are made explicit, and students demonstrate understanding through problem solving and design. The Circle Design Challenge is the culminating artifact.

  • (Plan circle vocabulary and theorems early; scaffold chord and tangent investigations)
  • (Coordinate labs on radians and arc/sector calculations with technology and manipulatives)
  • (Circle Design Challenge: final project; allow multiple sessions for design and presentation)
  • (Integrate SP and DM review cycles to reinforce key concepts)

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Circle vocabulary; central vs. inscribed angles; arc measures
  • Tangent-line theorems; chord properties; cyclic quadrilaterals (select)
  • Radians, arc length, and sector area
  • Right-triangle structures hidden in circles
