üì¶ Module 0 ‚Äì Build: Patterns, Precision, and Reasoning

üéØ Overview

Module 0 establishes the foundations for the year during Weeks 1‚Äì5. Students build routines, tool skills, and diagram literacy, while developing precise vocabulary and habits for definition writing. The weeks focus on routines, vocabulary, angle measurement, and classic compass and straightedge constructions (including applications). Students connect midpoint using coordinate methods. The module culminates in IM 1.9, a real-world application challenge (celebration-style culminating task, light scoring emphasis), during Family Weekend, with group SP awards and celebration (shortened week with visitors; pacing adjusted for light, collaborative work). Emphasis is on building community, fluency with both physical and digital geometry tools, and micro-skills needed for later proof and transformation work.

  • Weeks 1‚Äì2: Routines, folder/notebook setup, seating, Skill Gates, DeltaMath intro, SP routines, discovery-based diagram exploration, formal vocabulary (point, line, plane, ray, segment, endpoints, coplanar, collinear, intersection, congruent, parallel, perpendicular, bisector, circle, center, radius), Flashcard Factory sessions.
  • Weeks 2‚Äì3: Diagram labeling, vocabulary expansion, Make Your Own / Match Mine instruction writing, ‚ÄúWhat makes a good definition?‚Äù activities, collaborative vocabulary building.
  • Weeks 3‚Äì4: Angle measurement and naming, classic compass/straightedge constructions (equilateral triangle, perpendicular bisector, angle bisector), diagram micro-skills.
  • Week 4‚Äì5: Coordinate midpoint connection, IM 1.6 construction stations, consolidation of all skills, IM 1.9 performance challenge, Family Weekend, and group SP awards.

  • Diagram literacy: interpreting, labeling, and marking diagrams; naming conventions for points, lines, rays, segments, angles, triangles, and polygons; reading congruence (‚âÖ), parallel (‚à•), perpendicular (‚üÇ), right-angle squares, and other standard marks; naming polygons in vertex order; naming congruent objects in the same order.
  • Vocabulary precision: formal definitions for core geometry terms (point, line, plane, ray, segment, endpoints, coplanar, collinear, intersection, congruent, parallel, perpendicular, bisector, circle, center, radius) and early shape vocabulary (polygon, triangle types, quadrilateral types, vertex, side, interior/exterior); introduction to regular polygon; adjacent/vertical angles, linear pair, complementary/supplementary, transversal, and angle position terms introduced for familiarity; meta-vocabulary (example/non-example, counterexample, classify/differentiate, necessary/sufficient, legal/illegal move, equidistant, constraint, assumption, precision).
  • Definition writing: classify/differentiate method using examples and non-examples; collaborative drafting and peer testing; refining for necessary & sufficient conditions; adding polygon types as full dictionary entries (Week 4 warm-up).

  • Angle construction applications: design contexts (tiling, motifs) and mapping contexts (perpendicular bisector as set of equidistant points); link to real-world and cultural geometry; discussion of visual reasoning as a universal tool.
  • Use of both physical and digital tools for constructions: compass, straightedge, pencils, DeltaMath interactive constructions; optional GeoGebra introduction.
  • Coordinate midpoint connection: midpoint formula and segment partitioning; link to perpendicular bisector concept; notebook call-out linking midpoint ‚Üî equidistant ‚Üî perpendicular bisector.
  • Gate-ready micro-skills: naming conventions, diagram interpretation, vocabulary use in justifications, coordinate midpoint calculation and interpretation; readiness checks via DeltaMath Skill Gates.
  • SP routines and group recognition: peer coaching (green pen), visible revisions (color-swap), tidy tape-ins, SP highlights, table awards; equity through rotating group roles.

Compass/straightedge fluency

  • Philosophy: Constructions are a thinking gym, not a perfection test. The goal is exposure, geometric intuition, and recognizing when/why a construction solves a problem. Precision is encouraged but not required for mastery credit.
  • Structure: Each construction is completed twice‚Äîonce physically (compass/straightedge) and once digitally (DeltaMath) for a friction‚Äëfree guided run‚Äëthrough.
  • Coverage: Draw circle (fixed center/radius), straightedge through two points, transfer radius, perpendicular bisector, angle bisector, equilateral triangle, regular hexagon, parallel through a point, perpendicular through a point, and copy an angle.
  • Applications: Revisited in collaborative challenges where groups apply constructions in context. Reasoning & application take precedence over flawless execution.
  • Classroom culture: Productive struggle is welcomed. Students compare approaches and explain purposes/logic; success ‚â† perfect compass control.
  • SP & Assessment: SP for persistence, clear labeling, and reasoning. Skill Gates check understanding of purposes and diagram reading rather than demanding perfect physical replication.

üì¶ Module 1 ‚Äì Move: Transformations, Intersections, and Logic

üéØ Overview

Module 1 (Weeks 6‚Äì12) launches with rigid motions (transformations), intersecting lines and angle relationships (including vertical, adjacent, linear pair, and transversal relationships), and connections to coordinate algebra. Students explore translations, rotations, reflections, and symmetry, then use these tools to analyze transversals, parallel lines, and triangle angle sum. Formal proof writing is introduced, focusing on angle relationships and algebraic logic. The module culminates in a 3D Printed Tile Design project, synthesizing transformation and design thinking. Angle-type identification and algebraic angle puzzles live here and serve as the on‚Äëramp to formal angle proofs.

  • Weeks 6‚Äì8: Hands-on and Desmos-based transformation tasks (translations, rotations, reflections), symmetry, and diagram reasoning.
  • Weeks 9‚Äì10: Intersecting lines, angle relationships, algebraic angle puzzles, transversals and parallel lines, triangle sum theorem.
  • Weeks 11‚Äì12: Coordinate slope investigations, proof writing with angles and algebra, 3D Printed Tile Design project and reflection.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Algebraic angle puzzles (complementary/supplementary, vertical/linear pair, transversals)
  • Transversals and parallel lines
  • Triangle sum theorem and proof
  • Coordinate geometry: slope, parallel/perpendicular conditions
  • Introduction to formal proof writing (angles, algebra, transformations)

üì¶ Module 2 ‚Äì Prove: Congruence, Connections, and Argument

üéØ Overview

Beginning in Week 14 and running through Week 16, Module 2 focuses on triangle congruence proofs, congruence criteria, and the communication of mathematical arguments using multiple proof formats. Students develop fluency with SSS, SAS, ASA, and AAS (HL as time allows), mark and name diagram correspondences, and synthesize their learning before semester end. The module emphasizes careful diagram reading, proof mapping, and synthesis tasks.

  • Lessons: diagram reading, marking correspondence, proof vocabulary, scaffolded proof formats (flowchart, paragraph, annotated diagram).
  • Anchor activities and Skill Gates to support proof fluency; congruence card sorts, error analysis, and synthesis tasks.
  • Buffer days for remediation, extra practice, and check-ins before break.

üìö Skills & Topics

  • Triangle congruence criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS (HL optional as time allows)
  • Marking and naming correspondence for clarity
  • Proof formats: flowchart, paragraph, annotated-diagram
  • Auxiliary constructions to unlock proofs and connect to coordinate geometry

üì¶ Module 3 ‚Äì Similarity in the Wild

üéØ Overview

Students formalize dilations, develop triangle similarity criteria (AA, SSS~, SAS~), and use parallel-line proportionality to solve for unknowns in geometric and real contexts.   • Weeks 17‚Äì18: Dilations and similarity; triangle similarity criteria; proportional relationships among corresponding parts; parallel-line proportionality in triangles.

üì¶ Module 4: Trigonometry in Action

üéØ Overview

Students ground sine, cosine, and tangent in similar right triangles, then apply trig to solve missing sides/angles and model real contexts. The Miracle on the Hudson task and the Trig in Action poster project serve as central capstones. Pacing includes review days and buffers for absences. Presentations are optional for SP; top posters displayed.

  • Trig introduced after similarity; review days built in.
  • Miracle on the Hudson (mid-module); poster project at end.
  • SP routines and DeltaMath review for trig vocabulary and problems.

üìö Skills & Topics
