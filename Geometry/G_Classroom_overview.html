  • Classroom Manila Folder
    • Stored in the classroom.

    • Work flows: classroom folder (unfinished) ‚Üí mini folder (finished) ‚Üí notebook (final placement if taped in).

üåÄ Systems & Flow

  • Lesson Rhythm
    • Most lessons include:
      • Visual, hands-on, or investigative introduction to the concept.
      • Whole-class discussion to connect student observations to formal definitions and theorems.
      • Guided or independent notebook work to record key ideas and examples.
      • Warm-up and reflection prompts are a standard part of most lessons, with warm-ups taped in at the start and reflections completed at the end or on the following day.
      • From Thursday onward each week, students receive a daily color-coded warm-up/reflection handout at the start of class. Warm-ups are taped into the top of the day‚Äôs notebook page; reflection prompts are kept with a paperclip until used on a later day.
    • Vocabulary and definitions are introduced through guided class discussions and maintained on module-specific reference pages in the notebook.
  • Skill Points (SP) opportunities occur daily and are embedded into warm-ups, group work, and creative problem-solving tasks.
  • Students self-select SP-worthy work via Google Form on Fridays and submit a photo. On Mondays, selected examples are shown, and stamps are awarded for notable pages. SP stamps are only for outstanding design, creativity, or problem-solving.
  • Project-Based Emphasis
    • Many modules include a culminating mini project or poster (e.g., Indirect Measurement Poster in Symmetry & Similarity, Trig in Action in Right Triangle Trigonometry).
    • Projects are worked on in class with checkpoints, with top examples displayed at the end of the module.
    • SP can also be awarded for creativity, precision, or collaboration in project work.
  • Notebook as a Textbook
    • Students build a personalized record of definitions, theorems, examples, and reflections.
    • Tape-in handouts, foldables, and visual organizers reinforce conceptual connections.
    • Journal prompts encourage metacognition (e.g., ‚ÄúWhere have you seen this shape or relationship in the real world?‚Äù).
  • Assessment Structure
    • Practice: Ungraded, skill-building activities (DeltaMath, classwork, puzzles). Practice: daily or weekly as needed.
    • Skill Gates: Short, focused checks embedded within module progression to confirm procedural fluency.
    • Challenges: Culminating performance tasks or projects to demonstrate mastery in context at module or sub-module culmination, with time for feedback before final submission.
    • SP is not part of the grade but serves as a classroom engagement and motivation system, with stamps awarded for notable notebook pages or participation.
  • Spirals & Connections
    • Concepts recur across modules (e.g., similarity reappears in circles; triangle trigonometry resurfaces in modeling arc lengths).
    • Skills from Algebra 1 (coordinate geometry, proportional reasoning) are intentionally threaded in to prepare for SAT and higher math.
  • Role Assignment in Group Work
    • Group roles rotate regularly to balance collaboration. Roles will be finalized after initial weeks once natural table dynamics are observed.
  • Scoreboard & Group Rewards
    • A visible Classroom Screen scoreboard runs from Tuesday onward each week. On Fridays, all groups receive an SP boost, and the winning table gets a Treasure Box pick.
